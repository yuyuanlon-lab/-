During our seven-day journey to Peterfield, Jamaica, we had the opportunity to visit and learn from local construction material factories, including a concrete block factory and a welding workshop—both integral community-run enterprises. Additionally, we toured local construction sites, gaining insights into the region’s building processes and techniques.
Without removing the fence, the project transforms it into a commons by boarden the boundary through a process of pull and push walls. Selected sections of the walls are folded and reconfigured to create architectural spaces. The folded boundary creates openings that engage with the street, the inner garden, and the adjacent football field, allowing for a dynamic interplay between enclosure and openness.
All the materials and construction techniques—from the lightweight steel roof structure to the breeze blocks and concrete—were informed by close observation of and engagement with local workshops and building practices. Every element emerged from the local economy and the community itself. This is a truly community-rooted open space: shaped by the community in its design and construction, and ultimately serving the community in everyday life.
