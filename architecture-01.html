01

Walls of history

—
War Museum Design

School work, Design studio 2022

This project explores boundaries between interior and exterior, as well as between history and the present. The museum materialises these boundaries as a sequence of walls. The walls not only separate interior and exterior space, but also frame both objective and subjective ways of viewing history. At the same time, I explore the potential of the wall beyond its conventional role as a structural and dividing element. The walls can also become spaces in themselves, creating interstitial areas between rooms. In this way, the boundaries blur and the relationship between those dualities can be redefined.

The museum is located in the Yingxiong (Hero) Mountain Scenic Area, a memorial park commemorating the soldiers who died in the Jinan Campaign during the Chinese Civil War. The park is organized by two primary axes. The east-west axis connects the city to the mountain and is lined with public squares. The north-south axis connects the cemetery to the monument and passes through the forest. The museum site sits precisely at the intersection of these two axes.

The two axes suggest two ways of seeing history. By placing themselves within history, visitors can empathise with people in the past and experience the uncertainty of not knowing what lies ahead. Visitors who view history from a contemporary perspective can instead take a more rational and objective position.

I chose a sequence of walls as the architectural form because walls always present two sides. One side blocks the view and reads like a series of monuments along the path, while the other creates gaps that open views and establish connections from the urban area to the mountain. The walls separate inside from outside while also juxtaposing the past and the present.

The exterior circulation consists of three walkways that run along the walls toward the mountain, with corridors and staircases connecting the three gap spaces hidden inside the walls. Following this route, visitors can glimpse the exhibits from above and gradually understand the larger organization of the museum.

The interior circulation is more winding and complex, with a series of ramps moving underground and above ground. The route repeatedly passes through the walls and into exhibition halls devoted to different historical periods, creating an immersive experience of history.
