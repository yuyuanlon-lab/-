Bridge

The waterfront complex

Adapted from UIA-HYP Competition 2022

The building is a collection of paths that invite people to wander through the village, greenbelt, and along the river.

Bagua Island is a rural landscape of dispersed villages and agricultural fields, set within the broader urban context of Nanjing. A 31-kilometer riverfront greenbelt—composed of lawns and wetlands—encircles the island, separated from the villages by a two-way road and crossed by informal pedestrian and bicycle paths. A waterway links the city across the Yangtze River to the ferry terminal. The site is defined by three juxtaposed textures: village, greenbelt, and water. Before 2005, the area functioned as fishpond agriculture. Rapid industrialization after 2005 introduced shipyards along the river, severing the village’s connection to the Yangtze. Since 2018, environmental policies have removed these shipyards and restored the riverfront ecologically, while preserving the ferry terminal.

Different activities take place on the site. Both local villagers and vistors from urban area cross the river by ferry, making the small, shabby ferry terminal a busy place. Also, Buagua island is famous for wild vegetables, and people in Nanjing love to dig vegetable on the garsslands on the island. In villages, the market is the one of few public spaces where villagers can gather. These places and activities allow for a number of potential connections to emerge within the site, making attempts to bridge the gaps between different zones possible.

The architectural form is modelled on the curves that exist on the site, the existing paths, trails and the various possible circulations. These curves are brought together to form the outline of the building. The building is a collection of paths that link the various destinations around the site. In this way, architecture bridges the gap in the form of roads.
